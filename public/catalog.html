Lily of the valley

Lily of the valley's flowers are bell-shaped and droop to one side of the flowering stalk. Its red berries are poisonous. The round, green flower buds of lily-of-the-valley. Lily-of-the-valley is usually found in woodlands, favouring chalky soils.

Dahila

Dahlia is a genus of bushy, tuberous, herbaceous perennial plants native to Mexico and Central America. Dahlias are members of the Asteraceae family of dicotyledonous plants, its relatives include the sunflower, daisy, chrysanthemum, and zinnia.

Zinnia

Zinnia is a genus of plants of the tribe Heliantheae within the family Asteraceae. They are native to scrub and dry grassland in an area stretching from the Southwestern United States to South America, with a centre of diversity in Mexico

Poppy

Papaver is a genus of 70–100 species of frost-tolerant annuals, biennials, and perennials native to temperate and cold regions of Eurasia, Africa and North America. It is the type genus of the poppy family, Papaveraceae.

African Daisy

African daisies are vibrant, daisy-like flowers with a variety of colors, often with a darker center, known for their spreading growth habit and ability to bloom from spring to frost in cooler climates. They're great for adding a touch of color to gardens and containers, and are often used as ground cover or in rock gardens.

Azalea

Rhododendron is a very large genus of about 1,024 species of woody plants in the heath family. They can be either evergreen or deciduous. Most species are native to eastern Asia and the Himalayan region, but smaller numbers occur elsewhere in Asia, and in North America, Europe and Australia.

Chrysanthemum

Chrysanthemums, sometimes called mums or chrysanths, are flowering plants in the Asteraceae family. They are native to East Asia and northeastern Europe. Most species originate from East Asia, and the center of diversity is in China. Countless horticultural varieties and cultivars exist.

Blossom

Blossom is when flowers start popping open on plants or trees — kinda like nature showing off. It usually happens in spring when everything wakes up after winter. You’ll see trees covered in pink or white blooms, flowers opening up everywhere, and the air smelling nice and fresh. It’s that time when everything feels alive and pretty again.

Daffodil

A daffodil is that bright yellow flower that totally screams “spring is here!” It’s got a trumpet-shaped middle and petals around it, kinda like a fancy little sun in flower form. You’ll see them popping up in gardens, parks, and even along roadsides. Super cheerful vibes — like nature’s way of smiling after winter.

Amaryllis

Amaryllis is the only genus in the subtribe Amaryllidinae. It is a small genus of flowering bulbs, with two species. The better known of the two, Amaryllis belladonna, is a native of the Western Cape region of South Africa, particularly the rocky southwest area between the Olifants River Valley and Knysna.

Aster

Aster is a genus of perennial flowering plants in the family Asteraceae. Its circumscription has been narrowed, and it now encompasses around 170 species, all but one of which are restricted to Eurasia; many species formerly in Aster are now in other genera of the tribe Astereae.

Begonia

Begonias are those cool, kinda fancy flowers that come in all sorts of colors — pink, red, white, orange — and their leaves can look just as pretty as the blooms. Some are frilly, some are waxy, and they’re basically the drama queens of the flower world (in a good way). You’ll see them in pots, hanging baskets, or garden beds, just vibing and making everything look lush and alive.

Calla

Callas are those super elegant, smooth-looking flowers that look like they belong in a fancy wedding or some dreamy movie scene. They've got that sleek, curved shape — like a swirl or a little trumpet — and usually come in white, but you can find them in pink, purple, yellow, and even deep burgundy. They don’t scream for attention — they just quietly stun everyone with their classy vibes. Total flower royalty.

Carnation

Carnations are those ruffly, fluffy flowers that kinda look like nature took some tissue paper and made something cute out of it. They come in tons of colors — red, pink, white, yellow — and they last forever in a vase, so people love giving them in bouquets. They're not too flashy, but they’ve got that sweet, classic charm, like the reliable friend who's always there and somehow still looks great.

Delphinium

Delphiniums are those tall, elegant flowers that look like they’re reaching for the sky — kind of like floral skyscrapers. They come in dreamy shades of blue, purple, pink, and white, and when they bloom, they seriously steal the show in any garden. They’ve got that “majestic but chill” vibe — like the quiet kid in class who suddenly gives a brilliant answer.
